If you’re about to go into a manufacturing or production investment — buying machinery, building a facility, expanding capacity — one of the costliest mistakes is noticing the investment incentive certificate too late. A certificate obtained at the right time can bring the same investment down in cost noticeably. In this article I explain the logic from a manufacturer’s point of view.

## What is an investment incentive certificate for?

The certificate opens a series of support doors for your approved investments. Although it varies by the type, size and region of the investment, it typically includes:

- **VAT exemption on machinery and equipment.** The VAT burden on machinery purchases within the certificate’s scope is eliminated.
- **Customs duty exemption.** A customs-duty advantage on imported machinery and equipment.
- **Social-security employer premium support.** State support on the employer’s share of premiums, tied to employment.
- **Tax reduction and interest/profit-share support** and similar additional items (depending on region and investment type).

In short: you can bring the same machinery and the same employment to life at a significantly lower cost.

## The most critical rule: certificate first, investment second

I underline this. Most of the advantages the investment incentive certificate provides apply to expenditure made **after the certificate is obtained.** Saying “let’s also get an incentive” after buying the machine and having the invoice issued usually means being too late. That’s why, as soon as you make the investment decision — before you start spending — we need to sit down and talk.

## Not every investment is supported at the same rate

Support rates aren’t fixed; they vary according to the **region** where the investment is made, the **sector**, and whether the investment counts as **strategic/priority.** Two manufacturers setting up the same machine in two different provinces may not receive the same support. That’s why the answer to “how much does it gain me” requires a calculation specific to your project.

## An extra advantage for manufacturers: ease in VAT refunds

Some production businesses can also benefit from the VAT refund and offset opportunities that come with being a manufacturer. Combined with an incentive certificate, this can bring serious relief to the cash flow of a business entering production.
