“Should I set up a sole proprietorship or form a limited company?” — this is the question I hear most often from people starting a new business. The short answer: **it depends.** But below I’ve gathered the clear differences to make your decision easier.

## Sole proprietorship

**Advantages:**

- Quick and cheap to set up, and easy to close down too.
- Accounting and bureaucracy are simpler.
- Practical if you’re starting small, on your own.

**Disadvantages:**

- Liability is personal; a business debt binds you directly.
- As earnings rise, the progressive income-tax burden can get heavier.
- Corporate image and taking on partners are limited.

## Limited company

**Advantages:**

- **Limited liability:** As a rule, you take on risk only up to the capital you put in.
- Suited to forming partnerships and to growth.
- Provides a corporate image; some clients/institutions look for this.

**Disadvantages:**

- Formation and running costs are a little higher than a sole proprietorship.
- Accounting and obligations are more extensive.

## How should you decide?

Your answers to these three questions point the way:

1. **What will your turnover be?** As profit grows, the tax advantage of a limited structure can come to the fore.
2. **Will you take on partners?** If so, a limited/joint-stock company is more suitable.
3. **How much is your risk?** If business debt and liability could put personal pressure on you, limited liability becomes important.

This isn’t a matter of “right or wrong”; it’s a balance that depends on your business, your goals and your appetite for risk. When we sit down and look at it with the numbers, the answer usually becomes clear.
