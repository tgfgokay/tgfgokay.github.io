You’ve decided to grow your business, and now it’s time for the formal side. Setting up a company in Şişli — or any district of Istanbul — is actually a more orderly process than you might think, as long as you take the steps in the right sequence. Below I’ve summarised, in plain language, the path I walk my clients through as their accountant.

## 1. First, choose the right company type

Not every type suits every business. Broadly, there are three options:

- **Sole proprietorship:** Fast and low-cost. A sensible entry point if you’re starting small, on your own.
- **Limited company:** The structure SMEs most often prefer. A balanced option for those who want partnership and limited liability.
- **Joint-stock company:** If you’re planning to take on investors, transfer shares or build a more corporate structure.

This choice directly affects your taxation, your liability and your costs. Let’s definitely talk before you decide, because changing the type later can cost you both time and money. For the details, you can also look at the [sole proprietorship vs. limited comparison](/en/guide/sole-proprietorship-vs-limited/).

## 2. Prepare the required documents

Although it varies by company type, you’ll generally need:

- Identity and residence details
- A business address (a lease or title deed; a virtual office is possible for some activities)
- Articles of association (for limited/joint-stock companies)
- Signature declarations

I prepare most of the documents on your behalf; you only step in at the signing stage.

## 3. Registry and tax office procedures

For limited and joint-stock companies, formation is registered through the trade registry office. For a sole proprietorship, the tax office registration is done directly. If you’re going to operate in Şişli, your affiliated tax office and trade registry procedures run from here. I track all of these steps and complete them without a hitch.

## 4. Post-formation setup

The job isn’t done just because the company is open; the real order is built after this point:

- **E-signature / registered e-mail (KEP)** application
- Switching to **e-document** systems such as **e-invoice and e-archive**
- **Ledger certification** and setting up the accounting order
- Tracking the first **return and social-security** obligations

## How long does it take, how much does it cost?

If the documents are ready, a sole proprietorship is usually set up in 1–2 days and a limited/joint-stock company within a few business days. The cost depends on the type, the capital and the line of activity. I’ll draw up a clear, personalised breakdown for you in the initial call — no surprises.
